## COUNTER-INTELLIGENCE ANALYSIS

### What Aztec Is Actually Saying
Aztec claims Travis Ryle filed UCC-1 and UCC-3 financing statements asserting a security interest over Aztec's assets and receivables — specifically targeting public contract payments from Contra Costa County and Solano County.

### Key Factual Disputes (Travis Ryle's Position)

1. **Authorization Question:** Aztec claims no security agreement was ever executed. Travis Ryle's position is that the Trust holds valid interests based on its own legal framework.

2. **1099-A Filing:** Aztec claims this was fraudulent. Travis Ryle's position involves the Mortmain Vessel structure and the Trust's standing as a creditor entity.

3. **Frank E. Duarte Revocation:** The UCC-3 filing REVOKED Frank E. Duarte as authorized agent — this is significant because Duarte was the original registered agent of AZTEC CONSTRUCTORS, INC. This directly connects the Travis Ryle Trust's authority claim to Aztec's own corporate structure.

4. **Identity Question:** Aztec states it is "currently unaware of Defendants full legal name" — this is an admission that Aztec does not have complete information about the party it is suing.

5. **No Contract Produced:** Aztec admits it cannot produce any contract between itself and Travis Ryle — but the Trust's position is that the obligation arises from a different source (the UCC and commercial law framework).

### Strategic Observations

- Aztec is a California public works contractor receiving government funds from Contra Costa and Solano Counties
- The complaint was filed in Contra Costa County — where Aztec is based
- David J. Hyun (Aztec's attorney) is the same individual who appeared in OpenCorporates as connected to Aztec
- Aztec's complaint was filed July 2025 — AFTER Travis Ryle's federal civil rights case timeline (April 2024)
- The complaint does NOT address why Travis Ryle's Trust name appeared as an inactive officer/applicant in Aztec's own OpenCorporates record
- Aztec seeks to VOID all UCC filings and obtain injunction — this is defensive, not offensive, suggesting the filings created real legal pressure

### Open Questions for Counter-Investigation

1. How and when did THE TRAVIS RYLE PRIVATE BANK ESTATE TRUST first appear in Aztec's corporate records?
2. Was Frank E. Duarte acting in a dual capacity?
3. What is the nature of the SS Guarantee Assurance 104 0190 003558 referenced in the 1099-A?
4. What public contracts does Aztec hold with Contra Costa County and Solano County — and what is their value?
5. Is there any connection between Aztec's public contracts and the law enforcement/probation case against Travis Ryle?
6. Has the case been served? Has a case number been assigned?
7. Has Travis Ryle filed a response or cross-complaint?
